🎬 The Dreamers

People Who Built the Magic

As you scroll, meet the visionaries who turned a dream into the greatest storytelling empire on Earth.

01
👔
Co-Founder · The Businessman

Roy O. Disney

1893 – 1971

Roy Oliver Disney was Walt's older brother and the financial backbone of The Walt Disney Company — the man who made the magic possible in the real world. While Walt dreamed, Roy did the numbers, secured the loans, and kept the studio from collapsing during its most fragile years.

He personally negotiated the financing for Snow White when every banker in Hollywood called it "Disney's Folly." When Walt died in 1966, Roy postponed his retirement to see Walt Disney World completed, dedicating it to his brother's memory just 71 days before his own death.

Roy's quiet, steadfast dedication is the reason any of it exists at all.

📊 Financial Architect 🏗️ Built Walt Disney World 💼 55 Years of Service
02
✏️
Co-Creator · The Animator

Ub Iwerks

1901 – 1971

Ub Iwerks was Walt Disney's oldest friend and the artistic genius who literally drew Mickey Mouse into existence. When a rival studio poached all of Disney's animators in 1928, Ub single-handedly animated the entire first Mickey short, Plane Crazy, in just two weeks.

He was so extraordinarily fast he could produce 700 drawings a day — an output no other animator in history has matched. He later invented many of the multiplane camera and special effects techniques that define classic Disney films.

Without Ub's hands, there would be no Mickey, no Disney as we know it.

🐭 Drew Mickey Mouse 🎥 Invented Multiplane Camera ⚡ 700 Drawings/Day
03
🎨
Concept Artist · The Visionary

Mary Blair

1911 – 1978

Mary Blair was the colour soul of Disney's Golden Age. Her bold, brilliantly saturated style defined the look of Cinderella, Alice in Wonderland, and Peter Pan. Walt Disney called her his favourite artist and gave her an influence almost no other woman in Hollywood had in the 1940s.

She was one of the few women at Disney to travel on the 1941 South American research trip, and her sketches directly inspired Saludos Amigos. Her most visible legacy — the iconic 90-foot mural in Tomorrowland at Walt Disney World — still stands today.

Her fearless use of colour changed what animation was allowed to look like forever.

🎠 It's a Small World 🌈 Colour Visionary 👑 Walt's Favourite Artist
04
🎭
Nine Old Men · The Heart

Frank Thomas & Ollie Johnston

The Inseparable Duo

Frank Thomas and Ollie Johnston were two of Walt's legendary "Nine Old Men" — the core group of animators who defined the 12 principles of animation that every animator in the world still learns today. They were best friends for over 70 years.

Together they wrote The Illusion of Life: Disney Animation — the single most important book ever published about animation. Frank animated the dwarfs weeping over Snow White's coffin. Ollie gave Bambi his innocence and Pinocchio his longing to be real.

They didn't just animate characters — they gave them souls you could feel in your chest.

📚 Wrote The Illusion of Life 💔 Snow White's Grief Scene 🦌 Gave Bambi His Soul
★
🎩
The Dreamer · The Creator · The Man

Walt Disney

1901 – 1966

Walter Elias Disney was not just the founder of a studio — he was the architect of wonder itself. Born in Chicago in 1901, he failed at dozens of ventures before a little mouse changed everything. He had no formal art training. He was rejected by 302 banks before one said yes to Disneyland.

He made it anyway. Snow White, Fantasia, Bambi, Cinderella — he was personally involved in every frame. He pioneered television when Hollywood mocked it. He built Disneyland when everyone said a theme park for families was a foolish idea.

Walt held 32 Academy Awards — more than any individual in history. He built the most beloved entertainment company on earth from a garage, a borrowed $500, and a belief that has never faded: "It all started with a mouse."

🏆 32 Academy Awards 🐭 Created Mickey Mouse 🏰 Built Disneyland 🎬 Father of Modern Animation
"All our dreams can come true — if we have the courage to pursue them."
